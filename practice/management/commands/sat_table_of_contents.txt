## Part 2: Math Tactics & Test Strategy (20 Lessons)



On the Digital SAT, knowing the math is only half the battle. These 20 strategy lessons teach students how to hack the test, use the built-in Desmos graphing calculator, and leverage multiple-choice architecture.



81. **The "Plugging In Numbers" Tactic**: Replacing variables with simple constants ($2, 3, 10$) to solve abstract algebraic expressions.

82. **Backsolving (Working from the Options)**: Plugging answer choices back into the question, starting with middle values (B or C).

83. **Mastering Desmos I**: Graphing lines, curves, and finding intersections (intersections = solutions).

84. **Mastering Desmos II**: Utilizing the `slider` function to instantly solve equations with unknown constants.

85. **Mastering Desmos III**: Typing systems of inequalities to visually identify valid coordinate points in bounded areas.

86. **The "Eyeballing" Strategy**: Using geometry diagrams (even if labeled "not drawn to scale") to eliminate absurd geometry answer choices.

87. **The Art of Estimation**: Rounding messy decimals or complex expressions to quickly eliminate wrong options.

88. **Strategic Guessing and Time Management**: How to safely skip a time-sink question and return later without ruining your pacing.

89. **Avoiding the "Trap Answers"**: Identifying and dodging common trick choices (e.g., solving for $x$ when the question asks for $2x+5$).

90. **The Grid-In Blueprint**: Rules for Student-Produced Responses (handling fractions, decimals, and preventing formatting errors).

91. **Translating English into Math**: A dictionary of key terms ("is" $\to =$, "of" $\to \times$, "product" $\to \times$, "at most" $\to \le$).

92. **Recognizing Structure**: Solving massive expressions instantly by treating a recurring grouped expression as a single variable (e.g., if $3x+2 = y$).

93. **The Extreme Plug-In Technique**: Using $0$, $1$, or negative numbers to test constraints in tricky inequality or conceptual function problems.

94. **The Direct Translation Method for Word Problems**: Reading long prompts sentence-by-sentence to construct mathematical models on the fly.

95. **Using the Scratchpad Effectively**: Minimizing cognitive load by tracking intermediate steps on paper instead of doing mental math.

96. **The "Testing the Boundaries" Tactic**: Evaluating function limits or extreme values to find domain and range restrictions.

97. **Data Table Extraction**: Skimming long word problems to find hidden numerical constants tucked away in charts.

98. **The Formula Sheet Hack**: Recognizing which geometric formulas are readily available in the digital interface so students don't waste memory.

99. **Pacing for Module 2 (Hard vs. Easy)**: Tactics for handling the adaptive nature of the test based on performance in Module 1.

100. **The Final Review Protocol**: The systematic 30-second double-check strategy to eliminate silly arithmetic mistakes before submitting.
